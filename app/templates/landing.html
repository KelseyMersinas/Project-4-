Welcome to our Mushroom Classification App!

With over 14,000 mushroom species found around the globe, from delicious edible varieties to potentially deadly ones, knowing which mushrooms to forage is essential for safe consumption. Our Mushroom Identification App uses the power of machine learning to quickly and accurately classify mushrooms based on their unique characteristics. Whether you're an experienced forager or just starting out, our app provides valuable insights right in the field. With user-friendly features and a comprehensive database, you can confidently explore and build your knowledge about the fascinating world of mushrooms. Join us on this journey to enhance your foraging experience while prioritizing safety!

This app can be used to get more information on what species of mushroom you may have found! Head over to our Identify page and enter in the description of your mushroom - observe the color, shape, size and features and report to our app your findings to get the best guess of it as either safe to eat or if it may be poisonous.
Always use safe practices when consuming food found in the wild. This app is for educational purposes only and you should always confirm with an expert before eating anything growing outside of a controlled environment.

One is a harmless Button mushroom, the other is the deadly Destroying Angel. Can your eyes tell which is which?

Mushrooms can appear very different or very similar, but understanding the characteristics of mushrooms can help you determine the species, even for the untrained!

The gills are the stringy fibers underneath the cap that release the spores. The way the gills are attached can be used to determine the type of mushroom. Many poisonous mushrooms differ from their harmless counterparts in how their gills appear.

The most prominent feature - the mushroom cap - can vary in shape and features.

The actual height and size can be very different across different species, from very small to large. Some mushrooms don't even have gills or caps as we would think of them, so the size and color may be the only characteristics to use to distinguish them!
